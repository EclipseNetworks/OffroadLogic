Mechanical Driveline

Transfer Case States & Torque Distribution Logic

2H

High Range 2WD

Seal Surface Optimization

Power is delivered exclusively to the rear axle. The front hubs are disconnected to reduce mechanical drag and parasitic loss, maximizing fuel efficiency and minimizing driveline vibration on bitumen.

Logic Application

Standard highway cruising and dry city driving.

4A

4WD Automatic

Active Torque On-Demand

Utilises an electronically controlled clutch to monitor wheel slip. Torque is sent to the front wheels only when needed. Safe for all surfaces, including high-traction sealed roads.

Logic Application

Variable weather, wet roads, and light gravel tracks.

4H

High Range 4WD

Locked 50:50 Distribution

Mechanically locks the front and rear driveshafts together. This ensures a consistent 50% torque split between axles. Not for use on pavement as it causes driveline "binding" during turns.

Logic Application

Snow, loose dirt, and consistent off-road surfaces.

4L

Low Range 4WD

Torque Multiplication Logic

Engages a secondary gear set in the transfer case to multiply torque by a ratio (typically 2.5:1 or higher). Used for high-torque, low-speed maneuvers. Requires vehicle to be in Neutral to engage.

Logic Application

Extreme rock crawling, heavy recovery, and steep inclines.

Technical Alert: Driveline Bind

Operating in 4H or 4L on high-traction surfaces (dry bitumen) forces wheels to rotate at the same speed. During turns, inner and outer wheels must travel different distances; if locked, the drivetrain absorbs this stress, leading to potential gear failure and tire scrubbing.
